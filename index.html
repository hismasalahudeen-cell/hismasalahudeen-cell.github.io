About Me

Teacher. Designer. Lifelong learner.

"I believe every learner deserves a thoughtfully designed experience — one built on empathy, evidence, and genuine engagement with the material."

My journey into instructional design started in the classroom, where I spent years understanding exactly what it takes for a learner to truly absorb and apply knowledge. As a Pre-K teacher, clinical intern, and after-school educator, I built an instinctive understanding of learning gaps, motivation challenges, and the power of intentional design.

Today, I channel that classroom experience into learning design — collaborating with subject matter experts, applying frameworks like ADDIE and ARCS, and leveraging tools like Articulate 360 Storyline and Arcade Software to create engaging, performance-based training materials.

I'm currently completing my Master of Education in Instructional & Learning Design Technology at the University of Texas at Arlington, while interning at Fuel the Future Platforms where I'm translating technical procedures into learner-centered experiences.

Design Philosophy

The principles that guide every learning experience I create

🎓
Learner-Centered Design

Effective learning starts with understanding the learner — their context, motivations, and gaps. I apply the ARCS model and design thinking to build experiences that are relevant, confidence-building, and satisfying. Value is the most meaningful element: when learner needs are truly met, everything else follows.

💡
Cognitive Clarity

Inspired by Mayer's Multimedia Principles and Cognitive Load Theory, I design with restraint — coherence, signaling, and spatial continuity are not afterthoughts but core to every deliverable. Learning should feel effortless, not overwhelming, so the content itself can shine through.

👥
Community & Collaboration

Drawing on Vygotsky's constructivism and Bandura's social learning theory, I believe the best learning environments create space for shared meaning-making. Whether designing peer activities, discussion prompts, or collaborative projects, I build in connection as a feature — not a bonus.
